## The challenge

Glow & Co. came to us with a problem that is common among early-stage beauty brands: a product range good enough to compete with established names, and a digital presence that was nowhere near good enough to prove it.

The founders had been selling primarily through third-party marketplaces. Margins were being squeezed by platform fees, brand messaging was diluted by competitor listings sitting right next to theirs, and they had no real ownership of the customer relationship. The goal was clear — launch a D2C storefront that could stand on its own, build repeat purchase behaviour, and make the brand feel as considered and premium as the formulations behind it.

The deeper challenge was that Glow & Co. did not yet have a coherent brand identity. The logo was a quick early decision made before the product range took shape. The colour palette, typography, and visual language had never been formally defined. Before we could build anything, we had to establish what Glow & Co. actually looked like.

## Our approach

We started with a brand identity sprint. Working closely with the co-founders, our in-house studio [Digi Design](/) mapped out the brand's positioning — skin-first, clean, accessible without being mass-market — and translated that into a complete visual system.

The logo was redrawn from scratch: a mark built on softness and precision, avoiding the overused botanical clichés that saturate the beauty category. We defined a primary palette anchored in warm off-whites and a single confident accent drawn from the brand's hero product line. The typography pairing — a geometric sans for display, a humanist sans for body — was chosen to feel current without chasing trends. Every decision was documented in a brand guidelines deck the founders could hand to any future collaborator.

With the identity locked, we moved into the website build. The storefront was designed mobile-first given the audience demographic. We kept the navigation flat and the purchase path short — product discovery, detail page, cart, and checkout in as few taps as possible. Product photography direction was handled in tandem with the web design so that imagery and layout were composed together rather than retrofitted.

On the technical side, the site was built for speed and for SEO from day one. Structured data for products, clean URL architecture, and a content section for skin education gave the brand a foundation to rank for the ingredient and routine-related queries its audience was already searching.

The entire project — brand identity through to live site — was delivered in eight weeks.

## The outcome

The storefront launched to an audience the brand had been warming through social for several months ahead of go-live. The response validated both the brand work and the site architecture.

> **Stat —** The D2C storefront generated approximately ₹10L in its first quarter of operation, with an average order value notably higher than the brand's marketplace channel.

> **Stat —** Mobile sessions converted at a rate that exceeded the industry benchmark for beauty D2C in India, reflecting the investment in a frictionless mobile purchase path.

Repeat purchase rates in the months following launch suggested that customers who found their way to the owned storefront were more engaged than marketplace buyers — they subscribed to the skin-education emails, returned for restocks, and referred within their networks. The brand now has a channel it controls entirely, with the margin headroom that comes with it.

The identity work has scaled cleanly too. Packaging extensions, social templates, and retail collateral have all been produced from the original guidelines without any visual drift — which is exactly what a well-constructed brand system should do.
