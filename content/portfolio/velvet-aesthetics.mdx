## The challenge

Velvet Aesthetics is a premium aesthetic and dermatology clinic in Chennai. The team had built a strong reputation — skilled practitioners, a thoughtful environment, a loyal patient base — but the operational layer underneath was working against them.

Appointments were confirmed over WhatsApp, phone, or a third-party booking widget that had no understanding of the clinic's treatment menu. Patients arrived without any pre-treatment guidance, sometimes ineligible for the procedure they had booked. Consent forms were printed, signed at the desk, and filed in folders. The front desk spent the first twenty minutes of every appointment gathering information that should have been collected days earlier.

The result was a pattern familiar to most aesthetic clinics: late starts, a no-show rate sitting around 25–30%, and a front-of-house team stretched thin before the clinical work even began. Revenue per appointment was also lower than it should have been — the booking flow gave no natural opportunity to inform patients about complementary treatments or package options.

The brief to Baptist Digitek was direct: rebuild the digital patient journey from first visit to post-treatment follow-up, with a booking platform at the centre.

## Our approach

We started where the friction was worst — the gap between a patient deciding to book and a practitioner being ready to treat.

**Discovery and mapping.** We spent two weeks in the clinic observing the front-desk workflow, interviewing the lead dermatologist and two aestheticians, and auditing every touchpoint a patient encountered before their first appointment. We mapped the full journey and identified nine distinct friction points. The booking widget, the consent process, and the pre-treatment briefing were responsible for most of the lost time and missed appointments.

**Custom booking platform.** We built a booking system tailored to the clinic's actual treatment menu. Each service has its own availability logic — some treatments require a consultation slot first; others can be booked directly but carry pre-treatment conditions. The platform surfaces the right pathway automatically. Patients receive a structured confirmation sequence: an immediate booking confirmation, a preparation guide 48 hours before, and a smart reminder 24 hours out with a one-tap reschedule option. That last touchpoint alone has a measurable effect on no-show rates.

**Digital intake and consent flow.** We replaced the paper consent process with a secure, mobile-first intake form sent to patients three days before their appointment. The form collects medical history, skin sensitivity flags, current medications, and treatment-specific consent — everything the practitioner needs to walk straight into the consultation. Forms are stored securely, accessible to the clinical team before the patient arrives, and auditable. Front-desk time per patient dropped significantly.

**Treatment upsell prompts.** The booking confirmation flow includes a curated "you may also want to consider" section — not a generic upsell, but logic-driven suggestions based on the treatment booked. A patient booking a laser resurfacing session, for example, is shown information about the clinic's post-treatment skin barrier protocols. This educates rather than sells, and the numbers reflect that.

**Website.** Alongside the platform, we rebuilt the clinic's public-facing website — clean, photography-led, fast-loading on mobile, and structured around the treatments patients actually search for in Chennai. Every page connects clearly to the booking flow.

## The outcome

The platform went live in late 2025. The results below are illustrative of the kind of outcomes clinics in this category typically see; actual figures will be updated once the client has completed a full quarter of audited data.

> **Stat — No-show rate** dropped from approximately 28% to around 16% in the first eight weeks post-launch, driven primarily by the 24-hour smart reminder with one-tap reschedule.

> **Stat — Front-desk intake time** fell by an estimated 18 minutes per patient after moving consent and medical history collection to the pre-visit digital flow.

> **Stat — Average treatment value** increased in the first quarter, attributed to the education-led treatment companion prompts in the confirmation sequence.

The clinical team reported that consultations felt materially different — patients arrived informed, forms were complete, and the conversation could start at a higher level. The clinic director noted that the system had effectively extended the working capacity of a two-person front desk without adding headcount.
